About Clearledgr

Finance lives in the inbox. We made the inbox work for finance.

Clearledgr is the coordination layer for finance teams. Invoices arrive in Gmail, approvals happen in Slack and Teams, and the books live in the ERP. We connect what's already there instead of asking your team to log into a new portal every morning.

Why we built this

The AP playbook hasn't changed in 20 years. An invoice arrives. Someone reads it. Someone matches it against a PO. Someone routes it for approval. Someone enters it into NetSuite or SAP. Someone follows up when it goes silent. The work is sequential, repetitive, and runs on inboxes — but every "modern AP" tool asks finance teams to abandon the inbox and rebuild their workflow inside a portal nobody opens twice a week.

We build where finance already works. The Box — the persistent, threaded home of an invoice from receipt to payment — lives in Gmail. Approvals happen on Slack or Teams cards because that's where finance asks for sign-off today. The ERP stays the system of record. Clearledgr is the coordination layer that makes them act like one product.

What we believe

Tools are render targets, not data homes.

Gmail is a work surface. Slack is a decision surface. The ERP is the system of record. None of them should pretend to be the system of truth for the work happening across all three.

Rules first, LLMs second.

Matching, validation, and state transitions are deterministic. The LLM writes summaries, drafts replies, and surfaces context — it never decides whether to pay an invoice. Reasoning is auditable; behavior is predictable.

Audit trails are not retroactive.

Every state transition writes an append-only row before the action completes. The trail is the truth, and it cannot be edited after the fact. That is how finance gets to trust autonomous behavior.

Build outbound, not inbound.

We sell to the mid-market and enterprise finance teams who already feel the cost of broken AP. We do not wait for prospects to discover us; we go to them.
